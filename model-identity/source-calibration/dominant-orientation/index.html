Dominant Orientation

Dominant Orientation identifies the strongest current pattern in a Source Calibration result. It shows which orientation is most active in the answers at the time of calibration.

How to read it

The label describes the strongest orientation signal, while the underlying score determines the status indicator. A label does not automatically mean the result is complete or stable; the score controls the color logic.

Why it matters

The model uses Dominant Orientation to understand the user’s current reflective stance and to choose a safer starting style for prompts, pacing, and cognitive support.

Important boundary

Dominant Orientation is not a personality type, diagnosis, or fixed identity. It is a private calibration signal that may change when the user recalibrates.
