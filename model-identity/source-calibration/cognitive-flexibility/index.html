Cognitive Flexibility

Cognitive Flexibility describes how readily a person can shift perspective, update interpretation, adapt to new information, and hold uncertainty without losing coherence.

How to read it

The result label summarizes the current flexibility pattern. The supporting score is shown on a 0 to 10 scale and determines the status indicator.

Why it matters

The model uses Cognitive Flexibility to adjust how it offers reframing, alternative interpretations, problem-solving paths, and reflective prompts.

Important boundary

Cognitive Flexibility is not an intelligence score or clinical measure. It is a private calibration signal that helps the model support adaptive reflection.
